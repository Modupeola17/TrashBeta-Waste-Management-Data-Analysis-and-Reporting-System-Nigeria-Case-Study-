# TrashBeta: Waste Management Data Analysis and Reporting System (Nigeria Case Study)

## Overview

Nigeria generates over 32 million metric tons of waste annually, yet waste collection systems remain inefficient, fragmented, and largely unaccountable. In urban centers such as Lagos, thousands of tons of waste go uncollected daily, creating environmental, health, and infrastructural risks.

TrashBeta is a data-driven project that evaluates the feasibility of a centralized digital waste reporting platform. The project leverages citizen-reported data to uncover patterns in waste management failures, assess public readiness for digital reporting, and provide actionable insights for operational and policy improvement.

---


## Problem Statement

Waste reporting in Nigeria operates across disconnected channels, including physical visits to local government offices, phone calls to waste operators, and informal complaints on social media.

This fragmentation results in:

- Lack of centralized reporting records  
- Limited visibility into problem locations  
- No structured resolution tracking  
- Weak accountability for service providers  

These challenges lead to persistent waste issues and reactive decision-making.

---

## Objectives

The primary objective of this project was to validate the demand for a digital waste reporting platform using real citizen-generated data.

Secondary objectives:

- Collect at least 300 verified waste reports  
- Achieve broad geographic coverage across Nigeria  
- Measure willingness to adopt digital reporting tools  
- Identify waste management patterns and hotspots  
- Validate findings using secondary data sources  

---

## Data Collection

- Collection Period: December 19–31, 2025  
- Total Responses: 301  
- Geographic Coverage: 25 states  
- Focus Area: Lagos State (5 LGAs)

Data was collected using a structured digital survey. Secondary validation was conducted using social media data.

## Data Summary

The dataset includes:

- Waste type and description  
- Location (State and LGA)  
- Issue duration  
- Urgency level  
- Previous reporting attempts  
- Community impact  
- User engagement indicators  

This enables both operational analysis and behavioral insights.

---

## Key Variables

**Independent Variables**
- Waste type  
- Location (State, LGA)  
- Issue duration  
- Previous reporting history  
- Photo willingness  
- Update preference  
- Future app interest  

**Dependent Variables**
- Urgency level  
- Community impact type  
- Engagement indicators  

---

## Analytical Approach

The analysis was structured across four key areas:

- Issue distribution and severity  
- Geographic concentration and hotspot detection  
- System responsiveness and reporting effectiveness  
- Citizen engagement and readiness for digital tools  

---

## Key Findings

- Waste issues are highly concentrated in specific LGAs, with Alimosho identified as a major hotspot  
- Many issues persist over time, with evidence of prior failed reporting attempts  
- Over 80 percent of respondents are willing to adopt a digital reporting platform  
- Trust and privacy concerns affect willingness to share photos and contact details  
- Waste is strongly perceived as a public health issue

## Tools and Technologies

- Data Collection: Google Forms  
- Data Analysis: Python (Pandas) / Excel  
- Data Visualization: Power BI / Tableau  
- Documentation: Notion / Microsoft Word  

---

## Limitations

- No image data due to platform constraints  
- Self-reported location data may contain inconsistencies  
- Sample is biased toward digitally active users  
- Short data collection period limits trend analysis  
- Rural populations are underrepresented  

---

## Recommendations

- Develop a centralized waste reporting platform  
- Enable anonymized reporting to improve trust  
- Implement performance tracking for waste operators  
- Use hotspot data for targeted interventions  
- Establish feedback mechanisms for users  

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how citizen-generated data can support more effective and accountable waste management systems.

By transforming fragmented reports into structured insights, TrashBeta highlights the potential for data-driven decision-making in addressing urban challenges.
